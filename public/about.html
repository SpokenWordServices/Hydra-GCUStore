About our digital repository, Hydra

The Hydra repository is the electronic archive for the University of Hull. It has been developed to hold, manage and preserve the growing amount of digital material generated by the research, education and administrative activities of the University. Current Hydra content includes:

  • Student course and module handbooks
  • Learning and teaching materials
  • Past exam papers
  • Doctoral theses
  • Open access papers published in peer-reviewed journals
  • Research-generated datasets
  • Collections of photographic and other images
  • Texts of past non-departmental lectures
  • Powerpoint presentations from past non-departmental lectures and talks
  • Benchmark role profiles and generic job descriptions
  • University committee papers
  • University codes of practice, policies and regulations

Hydra is particularly well suited to the management of materials that can be shared (openly or on a restricted basis) and/or where the material needs to be preserved for the medium to long-term. We encourage all university research, teaching and administrative staff to consider depositing documents with us and are always happy to discuss adding new categories of material to the repository.


Feedback and suggestions for future development are welcome: please email Chris Awre. Examples of possible content for the future include conference papers, book chapters, and other forms of written research output from academic staff and postgraduate students. In addition to textual material and images, Hydra can provide a home for sound files and software. The browser page is but one of a number of ways in which material in the repository can be accessed: search and direct linking from within eBridge or other websites, for example, are two more. Please explore the available options and check back for latest additions over the coming months.


The Hydra repository is provided by Library and Learning Innovation working in close co-operation with the Information and Communication Technology Department and the academic community of the University of Hull.
